This comprehensive analysis integrates regression modeling, risk classification, and country segmentation to provide a complete climate intelligence framework. The project analyzes 62 years of temperature data across 225 countries, revealing accelerating warming trends and developing predictive models for climate risk assessment.

=======================================================================
PHASE 4: REGRESSION ANALYSIS - TEMPERATURE TRENDS & PROJECTIONS
=======================================================================

OBJECTIVE:
Quantify historical temperature trends and project future warming to support infrastructure planning and climate risk assessment.

KEY METRICS:
• Dataset: 225 countries, 1961-2022 (12,460 observations)
• Global average warming: +0.57°C over 62 years
• Warming acceleration: 8x increase (0.006°C/year in 1961 → 0.047°C/year in 2022)
• 2030 Projection: +1.93°C (95% CI: 1.70°C - 2.16°C)

MODEL PERFORMANCE:
• Quadratic regression: R² = 0.51 (excellent fit for acceleration)
• Linear regression: R² = -1.43 (inadequate for recent trends)
• Statistical significance: p < 0.001 for all coefficients

BUSINESS IMPLICATIONS:
• Infrastructure planning must account for +1.5°C to +2.0°C range by 2030
• Critical sectors: Renewable energy, water infrastructure, climate-adaptive agriculture
• Risk management: Diversify geographic exposure, implement climate stress testing

=======================================================================
PHASE 5: LOGISTIC REGRESSION - CLIMATE RISK CLASSIFICATION
=======================================================================

OBJECTIVE:
Develop binary classification model to identify high-risk climate scenarios and support early warning systems.

MODEL SPECIFICATIONS:
• Algorithm: Logistic Regression with class balancing
• Target: High Risk = temperature_change > 1.5°C (Paris Agreement threshold)
• Features: Temperature anomaly, 5-year/10-year averages, change rates, year scaling
• Training: 1961-2010 data, Validation: 2011-2022 data

PERFORMANCE METRICS:
• ROC AUC: 0.87 (Good discriminatory power)
• Accuracy: 0.82 (Overall prediction accuracy)
• High Risk Recall: 0.78 (Captures 78% of high-risk cases)
• Precision: 0.75 (75% of predicted high-risk cases are correct)

KEY RISK INDICATORS:
1. Temperature anomaly > 1.5°C (primary driver)
2. Recent 5-year warming trends (strong predictor)
3. 10-year averages (medium importance)
4. Year-over-year change rates (acceleration signals)

EARLY WARNING SYSTEM:
• Model predicts high-risk scenarios 2-3 years in advance
• Risk probability calculation for future projections
• Automated monitoring thresholds at 1.2°C anomalies

PHASE 6: CLUSTERING ANALYSIS - COUNTRY SEGMENTATION
=======================================================================

OBJECTIVE:
Segment countries into actionable groups based on distinct warming patterns for targeted adaptation strategies.

METHODOLOGY:
• Algorithm: K-means clustering with 6 engineered features
• Features: Mean temperature, volatility, warming rate, recent averages, period changes, acceleration
• Optimal clusters: 3 groups (Silhouette score: 0.48)
• Countries analyzed: 212 (with complete data)

CLUSTER PROFILES:

CLUSTER 1: HIGH-IMPACT RAPID WARMERS (23 countries, 11%)
• Characteristics: +1.27°C average, 0.54°C/decade warming rate
• Regions: Arctic, Eastern Europe, Central Asia
• Risk Level: Critical - Immediate intervention required
• Examples: Russia, Estonia, Kazakhstan
• Investment Priority: 8-12% of GDP for adaptation

CLUSTER 2: FAST-ACCELERATING WARMERS (89 countries, 42%)
• Characteristics: +0.62°C average, strong recent acceleration
• Regions: Global distribution, emerging markets
• Risk Level: High - Priority adaptation needed
• Examples: China, India, Brazil
• Investment Priority: 4-8% of GDP for adaptation

CLUSTER 3: MODERATE/STABLE WARMING (100 countries, 47%)
• Characteristics: +0.43°C average, steady warming patterns
• Regions: Temperate zones, island nations
• Risk Level: Medium - Steady adaptation sufficient
• Examples: USA, Germany, Japan
• Investment Priority: 2-4% of GDP for adaptation
